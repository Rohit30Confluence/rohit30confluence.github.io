The story

From zero to here

Life started with cries — but since then, no time for that. There's always something to build, something to learn, something to break and fix again.

Everyone does engineering in four years. I did it in six — not because I was slow, but because I lived it. Work, earn, learn — every concept had a real-world counterpart. Every lecture had a bill attached to it.

When COVID hit, I didn't stop. I waved back at technology. From electricity to the internet, from manual scripting to AI, from copy-pasted projects to building everything from scratch. Every disruption became a direction.

From operations to services, founding teams to catching bad actors, resolving hard problems that open source communities wrestle with — that's been the career. Not a straight line. A real one.
